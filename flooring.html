SPC vs LVT: Technical Breakdown

Understanding the core differences between Merrinton's SPC Rigid Core and LVT ranges to help you choose the right flooring for your project.

🪨 SPC (Stone Plastic Composite) – The "Rigid Core"

The modern powerhouse. Designed to be almost indestructible and is the preferred choice for kitchens, bathrooms, and commercial spaces.

  • Core Material 70% Limestone / Virgin PVC
  • Thickness 6.0mm (incl. underlay)
  • Wear Layer 0.55mm Commercial Grade
  • Installation Unipush™ Click System
  • Waterproof 100% Fully Waterproof
  • Stability High - Temperature Resistant
  • Impact Resistance Excellent - Dent Resistant
  • Subfloor Tolerance Bridges minor imperfections

🧱 LVT (Luxury Vinyl Tile) – The "Flexible Choice"

The classic version of luxury vinyl. Known for its comfort, warmth, and wide variety of aesthetic patterns.

  • Core Material Flexible Multi-layer PVC
  • Thickness 2.5mm - 3.0mm
  • Wear Layer 0.30mm - 0.55mm
  • Installation Glue Down / Stick Down
  • Waterproof Water Resistant
  • Stability Moderate - Requires Flat Subfloor
  • Comfort Softer, Warmer Underfoot
  • Sound Absorption Excellent - Quieter Walk

✅ Which Should You Choose?

Choose SPC Rigid Core if you are installing in areas with temperature fluctuations (like conservatories), high moisture (bathrooms, kitchens), or over subfloors that aren't perfectly smooth. Its rigid stone core prevents "telegraphing" of imperfections and won't warp in direct sunlight or extreme temperature shifts.

Choose LVT for a softer, quieter feel underfoot in bedrooms and living rooms. It is ideal for large projects where a low-profile, permanent bond to the subfloor is required, and where you want maximum texture depth and realistic embossing.
